# Hypothesis Test Notes

## Objective
The aim was to evaluate whether the Treatment onboarding experience led to a measurable improvement in the Paid Conversion Rate when compared against the Control group.

---

## Test Inputs

**Primary Metric:**

* Paid Conversion Rate

**Control Group:**

* Users: 693
* Converted Users: 22
* Conversion Rate: 3.17%

**Treatment Group:**

* Users: 715
* Converted Users: 50
* Conversion Rate: 6.99%

---

## Statistical Test

**Test Used:**

* Two-Sample t-Test Assuming Unequal Variances

**Significance Level:**

* Alpha = 0.05

**Test Output:**

* t Statistic = -3.2801
* P-value (One-Tail) = 0.00053
* P-value (Two-Tail) = 0.00107

---

## Decision Rule
If the resulting p-value falls below 0.05, the Null Hypothesis is rejected.

---

## Result
The obtained p-value (0.00107) was below the defined significance threshold (0.05).
As a result, the Null Hypothesis was rejected.

---

## Business Interpretation
The Treatment group demonstrated a notably higher Paid Conversion Rate compared to the Control group.

**Control Conversion Rate:**

* 3.17%

**Treatment Conversion Rate:**

* 6.99%

**Observed Improvement:**

* 120.5%

The outcome is statistically significant, indicating the difference is highly unlikely to be a product of random chance.

---

## Conclusion
The experiment delivers strong statistical evidence that the redesigned onboarding experience positively impacts user conversion performance.
The hypothesis is supported by the data. The Treatment experience is recommended for broader rollout, with continued monitoring of refund rates and support ticket volumes advised.
